Vijay Stambh

Vijay Stambh, also known as the "Tower of Victory," is a historic monument located in Chittorgarh, a city in the Indian state of Rajasthan. It stands as a symbol of Rajput valor and the victory of Maharana Kumbha over the combined armies of Malwa and Gujarat.

Historical Significance:

Vijay Stambh was constructed in the 15th century, specifically between 1440 and 1448, by Maharana Kumbha, a prominent ruler of the Mewar dynasty. It was built to commemorate his victory in the 1437 Battle of Mandore.

Architectural Style:

Vijay Stambh is a fine example of Rajput architecture and is constructed primarily from red sandstone and white marble. It stands at a height of 37.2 meters (122 feet) and is adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures.

Structure and Design:

The tower is an impressive nine-story structure, each level featuring sculptures and inscriptions. The topmost level has an image of Lord Vishnu and other deities. The entire monument is adorned with images of Hindu gods, goddesses, and various forms of Lord Vishnu.

Cultural and Tourist Attraction:

Vijay Stambh is not only a historical monument but also a symbol of Rajput pride and valor. It attracts visitors interested in Rajasthan's history, architecture, and the bravery of its rulers. The monument has become a popular tourist destination in Chittorgarh.

Visiting Vijay Stambh:

Vijay Stambh is open to visitors. It's advisable to check for the latest information on visiting hours and any entry fees when planning a trip. Exploring the tower allows you to appreciate the historical and architectural significance of the region.


Vijay Stambh stands as a testament to the valor and spirit of the Rajputs in the face of adversity. It continues to be a symbol of pride for the people of Chittorgarh and a place where visitors can immerse themselves in the architectural and historical heritage of Rajasthan.
