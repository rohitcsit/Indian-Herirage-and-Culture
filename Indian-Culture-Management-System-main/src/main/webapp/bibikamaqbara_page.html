Bibi ka maqbara

Bibi ka Maqbara, often referred to as the "Mini Taj" or "Dakkhni Taj," is a historical monument located in the city of Aurangabad in the Indian state of Maharashtra. It is an architectural homage to the famous Taj Mahal in Agra and serves as the tomb of Dilras Banu Begum, the wife of Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb.

Historical Significance:

Bibi ka Maqbara was commissioned in the late 17th century by Prince Azam Shah, the son of Emperor Aurangzeb, as a tribute to his mother, Dilras Banu Begum. It was constructed between 1651 and 1661.

Architectural Style:

The monument is an example of Mughal architecture, with a distinct influence of the Persian architectural style. While it bears a resemblance to the Taj Mahal, it is much simpler and less ornate in comparison. The monument is constructed using locally quarried basalt rock and plaster, which gives it a different appearance from the white marble of the Taj Mahal.

Structure and Layout:

Bibi ka Maqbara features a central tomb structure with four minarets at its corners, a garden, a mosque, and a reflecting pool in the front. The main mausoleum is square in shape and has a central dome, which is flanked by smaller domes. The exterior is adorned with intricate floral and geometric designs.

Differences from the Taj Mahal:

While Bibi ka Maqbara is often compared to the Taj Mahal, there are notable differences. The Taj Mahal is larger, made of white marble, and is considered more exquisite in its architectural details and decorations. Bibi ka Maqbara, though inspired by the Taj, has a simpler and less intricate design.

Cultural and Tourist Attraction:

The monument is a popular tourist destination and a symbol of cultural heritage in Aurangabad. It attracts visitors interested in Mughal history, architecture, and art.

Conservation and Restoration:

Over the years, Bibi ka Maqbara has undergone various restoration and conservation efforts to preserve its historical and architectural value.

Visiting Bibi ka Maqbara:

Bibi ka Maqbara is open to visitors, and it's advisable to check for the latest information on visiting hours and any entry fees when planning a trip.


Bibi ka Maqbara is not just a historical and architectural monument but also a symbol of the enduring legacy of the Mughal Empire in India. It reflects the love and tribute of a son to his mother and continues to attract tourists from around the world who come to appreciate its architectural charm and cultural significance.
